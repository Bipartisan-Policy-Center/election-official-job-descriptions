=== JOB DESCRIPTION ===

The Larimer County Clerk & Recorder Elections division offers an outstanding opportunity for an exciting career in the always engaging field of Election Administration – where the foundation of government begins for our citizens!

We are seeking skilled Elections Technicians/Elections Specialists to join our highly respected team. We serve a population of more than 300,000 citizens, of which more than 250,000 are registered voters. We embrace innovative processes and have a shown reputation for integrity.

If you are a self-motivated, positive teammate who thrives in a fast-paced professional environment – we want to hear from you! The successful candidate will be dedicated, assertive, and possess outstanding interpersonal and problem-solving skills. The Elections Technician/Elections Specialist position provides support to and/or oversight for certain processes.

Specifically, this position:


Assists with incoming and outbound mail processing, customer support, revenue handling, and customer transactions.
Provides Voter Registration support services, including entering Voter Registration forms and other data transactions accurately and completely.
Reviews and updates Elections Office procedures yearly or as needed.
Monitors, analyze, and explain Election statutes and rules, including statutes and rules proposed by the Colorado State Legislature and the Colorado Department of State.
In addition to year-round daily tasks, support one or more of the following, Election Cycle process areas:

Coordinates with local entities to sign Intergovernmental Agreements, receive certified ballot content, and layout ballot artwork in the Election Management System.
Recruits, trains, and handles temporary employees (Election Judges) for job assignments at Voter Service and Polling Centers (VSPC) and at the Central Count facility.
Organizes and prepares outgoing mail ballot materials. Collaborate with print/mail vendor and United States Postal Service to achieve timely transmission and delivery of ballots to eligible voters.
Supports VSPC operations, including set-up, operation, and break-down of all VSPC locations, intense daily support for 500+ VSPC Election Judges, and end-of-day reconciliation.
Oversees the receipt, processing, verification, and tabulation of mail ballots in the counting facility. Provide direct supervision for 150+ Central Count Election Judges.
Ensures integrity of all Election process areas through enforcement of strict security, the chain of custody, and bipartisanship requirements.

We are looking for a candidate that will:
Have knowledge of general office practices and procedures. Along with the ability to use routine software and business application including, word processing, spreadsheets, presentation software, and databases.
Communicate clearly and concisely, both verbally and in writing.
Read and comprehend department and County rules, regulations, policies, standard operating procedures, and elections statutes and rules.
Be dependable and have regular, predictable attendance.
Multi-task with contending priorities and responsibilities while having the ability to work in a stressful, fast-paced, deadline-driven environment.
Work collaboratively with others from a diverse group of backgrounds in teams to ensure a successful project outcome.
Problem solve and troubleshoot complex systems.
Have supervisory, training, and project management experience.

All candidates will be screened for the following required minimum qualifications. Any candidate who does not meet all of the qualifications listed as required for an Elections Technician will be eliminated from the process. 

Depending on qualifications this position may be hired as Elections Technician or Elections Specialist. 

Elections Technician 


Two (2) years of office experience or in the performance of similar duties required.
High School Diploma or GED required.
Possession of, or ability to obtain, voter's registration in the State of Colorado within one (1) month of hire, is required.
Possession of a valid Driver's License by date of hire is required.
Possession of, or ability to obtain, within one (1) year, Colorado State Election Certification and/or maintain continuing education hours annually or as required.
Must be available to work evenings, weekends, and some holidays.
Elections Specialist 

Two (2) years of Elections experience required.
Qualify via the Elections Department scoring rubric for classification as an Elections Specialist.
High School Diploma or GED required.
Possession of, or ability to obtain, voter's registration in the State of Colorado within one (1) month of hire, required.
Possession of a valid Driver's License by date of hire required.
Possession of, or ability to obtain, within one (1) year, Colorado State Election Certification and/or maintain continuing education hours annually or as required.
Must be available to work evenings, weekends, and some holidays.
