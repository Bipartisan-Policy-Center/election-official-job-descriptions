Program Manager-Overseas Voting Initiative
The Council of State Governments (CSG) is the nation's only organization serving all three branches of state government. CSG is a region-based forum that fosters the exchange of insights and ideas to help state officials shape public policy. This offers unparalleled regional, national and international opportunities to network, develop leaders, collaborate and create problem-solving partnerships. To learn more about CSG please visit our website at www.csg.org. We are currently seeking a Program Manager at our Lexington, KY national headquarters office.
JOB SUMMARY:
The Program Manager of CSG's Overseas Voting Initiative, funded through a cooperative agreement with the US Dept. of Defense (DOD) Federal Voting Assistance Program (FVAP), provides day-to-day management and oversight of the Initiative, including research and policy analysis of electronic absentee voting systems for military voters, and development and dissemination of educational policy programming and deliverables to state leaders in support of the cooperative agreement. The Program Manager works within CSG's Center of Innovation and in cooperation with CSG's policy and executive management teams as well as regional offices, affiliates and members to support, monitor and improve state elections processes for military and overseas voters.
ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES:
- Manages the day-to-day cooperative agreement with the US Dept. of Defense (DOD) as part of the Federal Voting Assistance Program (FVAP), including management/supervision of other personnel assigned to the project, including all project contractors.
- Manages the relationship with the funder to ensure a positive and collaborative partnership.
- Ensures all project deliverables reflect quality and are delivered on-time according to project timelines.
- Conducts research and policy analysis of electronic absentee voting systems for military voters with which they may cast votes in federal elections.
- Develops and deploys educational policy programming and deliverables to local and state leaders focused on other electronic systems to support military and overseas voters including an electronic voter registration and federal write-in absentee ballot systems for federal candidates, and other online absentee balloting delivery and information portals.
- Develops and disseminates other policy research deliverables, programming, initiatives and meetings in support of the cooperative agreement.
- Serves as a CSG resource and spokesperson on CSG's Overseas Voting Initiative cooperative agreement.
- Regularly provides briefings for CSG executive management team, Director of the CSG Center of Innovation and project funders/partners on work of the initiative.
- Manages and staffs the CSG's Overseas Voting Initiative, funded through a cooperative agreement with the US Dept. of Defense (DOD) as part of the Federal Voting Assistance Program (FVAP) and coordinates the activities of all project committees, working groups, research, deliverables and programming.
- Stay abreast of emerging trends and research within the elections policy area; convene forums, encourage dialogue, and commission research that frame and explores critical new issues relevant to elections and overseas voting; and build consensus and support relating to those issues both within and outside of CSG Center of Innovation.
- Develop and expand a knowledge base on elections issues, processes and programming, and other relevant policy topics and best practices. Works with CSG Communications staff to ensure that all CSG OVI projects maintain the CSG brand standards as well as ensuring all appropriate cobranding is vetted with the project funder.
- Forge and maintain positive relationships and partnerships with members of the election community including but not limited to legislators, secretaries of state, election officials, governors' offices., non-profits, federal agencies, academics and other associations operating in the elections space.
- Create the initiative's national advisory groups and provide administrative, logistical and technical support. to the initiative's national advisory groups.
- Develop and conduct wide-spread dissemination of policy and education deliverables tailored for specific federal and state policymaking audiences using a variety of vehicles including in-person meetings, CSG newsletter/magazine articles, webinars, publications, web content and social media
- Prepare and make timely and professional presentations to state and federal policymakers, members of the elections community, and to other national organizations.
- Provide in-depth research, review and evaluation of state and local election practices as they relate to interaction with and promotion of election services and access to Uniformed and Overseas Citizens Absentee Voting Act (UOCAVA) voters and the development of 'best practice' policy guidelines for state and local election officials.
- Deliver ongoing support to FVAP in its collection, analysis and evaluation of election performance indicators and data from UOCAVA grant recipients and the development of promotable and tangible lessons from state and local election activities.
- Manage the active content of the CSG Overseas Voting Initiative Internet website, including updates on related events, legislative tracking of state and federal activities, online resource library and project information and online databases.
- Participate in other CSG national, regional and affiliate meetings and programs as necessary.
- Conduct outreach to state officials, participate in identifying and supporting policymakers interested in CSG's Overseas Voting Initiative deliverables and programs.
- Share expertise with CSG's regional offices and affiliate groups as requested.
- Collaborate with regional staff as appropriate to provide CSG members with meaningful insights and information about CSG's Overseas Voting Initiative.
- Work with CSG logistics and registration team to plan, organize and conduct meetings by arranging for speakers, identifying topics, send invitations, producing supporting materials and preparing information.
- Other organizational duties as assigned.

- Strong research and writing skills including policy analysis as well as more general writing such as correspondence, memos and articles.

- Knowledge and demonstrated skills and abilities with management and direct supervision of staff.

- Demonstrated experience and knowledge of state and federal general policy issues, with specific emphasis on state and/or federal elections, uniform state laws and general state government public policy.
- Demonstrated experience and knowledge of personnel and budget management.
- Demonstrated experience in developing, implementing and managing externally supported policy projects and programming.
