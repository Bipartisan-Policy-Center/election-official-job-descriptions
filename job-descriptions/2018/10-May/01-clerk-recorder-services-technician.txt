=== JOB DESCRIPTION ===

Bargaining Unit: Local 2700 - General Clerical Unit 
 
The Contra Costa County Clerk-Recorder is currently recruiting for the position of Clerk-Recorder Services Technician, located in the Recorder's Division of the Clerk-Recorder-Elections Department, in downtown Martinez,
 
The Clerk-Recorder Services Technician is a technical position assigned to one of the specialized units of the Clerk-Recorder Division: Recording, Clerk Services, Imaging/Indexing and Archive/Warehouse Services.  This position performs technical and complex data entry and clerical that may include: fictitious business name, general clerk filings, notary, passport, marriage license application processing and marriage ceremonies, real property and other general document recordings, document indexing and/or general index verification, customer service desk and phone support, and perform database management in one or more database systems; and perform related work as required.
 
The ideal candidate will possess the following:
 

Knowledge and understanding of County and Recorder functions as well as how the units interrelate
Knowledge of the principles and practices of work organization and the ability to apply them in planning, coordinating and completing work activities to meet specific deadlines
The ability to operate personal computers and peripheral equipment, including knowledge of spreadsheet, word processing, and database management programs
Knowledge of codes and laws relating to County Clerk and Recording functions, and the ability to independently apply them 
Ability to work in a fast paced environment with shifting priorities and time lines
Excellent interpersonal skills, as the incumbent will interface with staff at all levels, as well as county officials and members of the public
Effective oral and written communication skills

All Units

Uses computer-based programs and equipment to perform a variety of functions, including designing reports, forms, envelopes, and instruction sheets, processing customer requests, on-line database research, data entry, cashiering, document indexing, correspondence, form processing, quality and accuracy verification
Performs complex and technical day-to-day functions of the unit
Answers questions from the public regarding complex issues, including laws and regulations governing clerk and recorder processes. This may be at the counter, over the phone or electronically
Assists with studies on division matters and prepares professional reports
Provides positive customer service, in person, on the phone or electronically
Identifies requirements and schedules adequate time to prepare necessary documents and/or supplies to meet deadlines according to appropriate codes and department's procedures
Implements and follows procedures to increase efficiency and accuracy of division processes

Sorts incoming mail, processes according to division policies and procedures
Monitors and strictly controls access to confidential information
Assures security policies and procedures are strictly followed at all times for the facility, as well as documents and/or bank note security paper
Assists with reviews of procedures to assure completeness and correctness
Assists with maintenance of the division desk manuals
Maintains files, materials and records related to the division
Performs basic routine office equipment maintenance and troubleshooting; coordinates calls for service and/or repairs

In addition to the above:

Recording Services
Examines complex real property or other documents presented for recording for completeness, accuracy and proper execution
Determines sufficiency for recording based on applicable California codes and division procedures
Rejects documents that do not meet recording requirements
Corresponds with document presenters, specifying reason(s) for recording rejection
Validates general index for accuracy and completeness
Enters and maintains data in proprietary database system
Assist with examination of maps presented for recording to determine sufficiency of completeness, accuracy and proper execution
Provides direct support to Clerk-Recorder Services Specialists performing public (counter) or most complex recordings

Clerk Services
Examines documents presented for filing for completeness, accuracy and proper execution
Determines sufficiency for filing based on applicable California codes and division procedures
Enters and maintains data in proprietary database system
Provides guidance and direction to the public or other agencies at the customer service information counter
Schedules weddings according to division policies and procedures
Provides copies of vital or real property records from public record databases according to applicable California codes and division policies and procedures
Issues marriage licenses according to applicable California code requirements and division policies and procedures
Issues oaths and witnesses signatures as Deputy Clerk according to applicable California codes and division policies and procedures
Performs analysis of supporting information to confirm sufficiency for Professional Registration applicants
Assists with the proper security process for ordering, receipt and storage of registered supplies (vital record bank note paper).
Follows destruction schedule, assists with timely destruction according to schedule

Imaging/Indexing and Archive/Warehouse Support
Assists with data entry, index verification and database maintenance of Clerk-Recorder records
Assists with redaction of documents to meet California State Social Security Truncation Program requirements
Prepares documents for imaging, enters data and images into database system
Assures image and information quality control is according to and meets California codes and division policies and procedures
Assists with maintenance of Clerk-Recorder archive and warehouse inventory
Follows division procedures to provide access to archive facility
Assures sufficient supply inventory is available for division operations
Follows destruction schedule, assists with timely destruction according to schedule
Produces archive copies of Real Property records and index, or other documents from digital database
Prepares customer requests for electronic records according to California codes and division policies and procedures

License Required: Possession of a valid California motor vehicle operator's license.  Out of state valid motor vehicle operator's license will be accepted during the application process.
 
Education:  Possession of a high school diploma, G.E.D. equivalency or high school proficiency certificate.
 
Experience: Two (2) years of full-time, or its equivalent, clerical or technical experience using spreadsheets, word processing and database management programs.  At least (6) six months of the required experience must have included performing duties related to technical clerk and recorder responsibilities for a County Clerk-Recorder's Office.

Note: The Clerk-Recorder Technician is a specialized classification requiring knowledge of the California Codes, regulations and laws related to County Clerk and Recorder Functions; general clerical experience will not be substituted for the required (6) months experience as noted above.
